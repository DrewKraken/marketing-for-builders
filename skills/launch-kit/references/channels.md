# Launch channels (in depth)

This expands the gate map in `SKILL.md`. Read the card for the channel you're posting to. Each card is: the **gate** (what to get right first), the **hard rules** (with sources — these are real and enforced), the **format**, **timing**, and the **do/don't** that separates a launch that lands from one that gets flagged.

The thread running through all of them: these communities reward a builder being plain and honest, and they actively penalize marketing. The rules below are mostly formalizations of that one fact.

---

## Show HN (Hacker News)

**Gate — the title.** `Show HN: Name – what it does`, in plain words, no hype. The title plus a link is the entire pitch in the feed; if it editorializes or hides what the thing is, it's dead on arrival.

**Hard rules** (from the official [Show HN rules](https://news.ycombinator.com/showhn.html) and [guidelines](https://news.ycombinator.com/newsguidelines.html)):
- **It must be something people can try.** "Show HN is for something you've made that other people can play with." Blog posts, sign-up pages, newsletters, and waitlists do **not** qualify — "those can't be tried out."
- **Make it easy to try, ideally without a signup or email wall.** If it isn't ready for people to use, don't post it yet.
- **Plain titles.** "Don't do things to make titles stand out, like using uppercase or exclamation points, or saying how great [it] is." No superlatives.
- **Never solicit votes.** "Please don't ask friends to upvote or comment. That's not ok on HN." Same for asking the thread.
- **Be present.** It should be something "you're around to discuss" — you answer comments in the thread, especially the critical ones.

**Format.**
- *Title:* `Show HN: Name – one plain clause on what it does` (an en-dash or colon separating name and description).
- *URL:* the live thing (or a repo with a runnable quickstart).
- *First comment (post it right after submitting):* 2–4 short paragraphs — what it does, the honest reason you built it, a line or two on how it works, and the feedback you're after. This is where context lives; the title can't carry it.

**Timing.** US weekday mornings (roughly 8–10am ET) tend to have the most readers; avoid weekends. Don't overthink it — a good post on a slow day beats a hyped post that breaks the rules.

**Do / don't.**
- Do: lead with what it does, link to something runnable, invite teardowns.
- Don't: superlatives, "revolutionary," emoji-stuffed titles, "please check it out and upvote," a marketing landing page as the link with no way to actually use the thing.

## Product Hunt

**Gate — the tagline.** ≤60 characters, verb-led, one clear benefit. It's the line that does the converting in the feed. "Automate your changelog in one commit" beats "The future of release notes."

**Hard rules / mechanics** (2025 launch guides; see sources below):
- **Tagline cap is 60 characters.** Lead with a verb and a concrete benefit; clarity beats clever.
- **Launch at 12:01 AM PT.** The day resets at midnight Pacific; the ranking window is a single PT day, so launching at 12:01 AM gives the full day to accumulate.
- **Post the maker's first comment immediately** — a short, personal intro: why you built it, who it's for, and one ask ("we'd love your honest feedback"). Keep it to **2–3 short paragraphs**, not one dense block — it's skimmed on mobile. Not "please upvote" — Product Hunt discourages vote solicitation just like HN.
- **Featured is everything and rare.** In 2025 only ~10% of launches get featured (the homepage surface that drives the traffic), and clarity/value messaging is what earns it — not gimmicks.

**Format.** Name; tagline (≤60); a gallery (the first image matters most); the maker's first comment ready to paste; a description in plain benefit language.

**Timing.** 12:01 AM PT, ideally Tue–Thu. Line up the people who already know you to engage *that day* (genuinely — not a vote ring).

**Do / don't.**
- Do: verb-led tagline, a crisp first image, a human maker comment, reply to every comment.
- Don't: a 60-char tagline wasted on your company name, a launch at noon, "upvote us!", gimmick slogans with no benefit.

Sources: [Product Hunt launch guide (fmerian/awesome-product-hunt)](https://github.com/fmerian/awesome-product-hunt/blob/main/product-hunt-launch-guide.md), [2025 launch strategy after the algorithm change](https://awesome-directories.com/blog/product-hunt-launch-guide-2025-algorithm-changes/).

## Reddit

**Gate — subreddit fit + a title that reads as a builder sharing.** Reddit removes and bans promotional posts faster than any other channel. The post has to belong in the subreddit and sound like a person, not a press release.

**Hard rules / norms** (2026 guides; see sources):
- **The 9:1 spirit.** The old "9 contributions per 1 promo" rule was retired for being too rigid, but the principle stands: be a genuine participant, not just a promoter. Moderators judge your whole account.
- **Post where self-promo is welcome.** r/SideProject (the most promo-friendly indie sub) and its/others' weekly "Share your project" threads; r/SaaS allows it in "Show and Tell Saturday." Read each subreddit's rules first — they differ and they're enforced.
- **Disclose.** "Full disclosure: I built this" goes a long way. Undisclosed self-promotion is the fast path to a ban.
- **Value first, link last.** Lead with the story or the lesson; the link comes after you've given something. A bare link with three words of text reads as spam.
- **Have an account with history.** A brand-new account dropping a link is the classic spam signature.

**Format.** Subreddit (and the rule you're complying with); a title that's a builder sharing ("I built X to solve Y — here's what I learned"); a body with the story/value and the link near the end; engage in the comments.

**Timing.** Weekday mornings US time for most tech subs; follow the subreddit's weekly-thread schedule where one exists.

**Do / don't.**
- Do: disclose, give value, pick the right sub, reply to everyone.
- Don't: cross-post the same ad to ten subs, post a bare link, use a fresh throwaway account, argue with critics.

Sources: [Reddit self-promotion rules 2026 (KarmaGuy)](https://karmaguy.io/en/blog/reddit-self-promotion-rules), [the 9:1 rule in 2026 (Teract)](https://www.teract.ai/resources/reddit-subreddit-marketing-2026).

## LinkedIn

**Gate — the hook.** Only the first ~140 characters (mobile) / ~210 (desktop) show before "…see more." Those first one or two lines do all the work; if they don't earn the click, nobody reads the rest and the post dies.

**Hard rules / mechanics** (2026 data; see sources):
- **The truncation window is ~140 mobile / ~210 desktop.** Write the hook to fit, and **keep it in one paragraph** — a blank line ends the visible snippet early, so a hard line break inside the hook cuts it off.
- **Early engagement drives reach.** The algorithm amplifies posts that get likes/comments in the first 60–90 minutes; a weak hook starves that window.
- **Link in the first comment, not the opening.** A link competing with your hook pulls attention before the reader is sold; the common practice is to put it in the first comment.
- **Sweet spot for the full post is ~1,300–2,000 characters** — long enough to tell a story, but the hook is what determines whether anyone expands it.

**Format.** Hook (1–2 lines, one paragraph) → the story (what you built, why, what you learned) → a soft CTA → the link in the first comment.

**Timing.** Tue–Thu mornings in your audience's timezone; be available to reply for the first hour.

**Do / don't.**
- Do: open with a specific, curiosity- or result-driven line; tell a real story; reply fast.
- Don't: open with "I'm excited to announce…", bury the hook under a line break, drop the link in line one, post and disappear.

Sources: [LinkedIn character limits 2026 (Lunatic AI)](https://www.lunatic-ai.com/guides/linkedin-character-limits), [LinkedIn character limits + best length data (AuthoredUp)](https://authoredup.com/blog/linkedin-character-limit).

## X / Twitter

**Gate — the hook post.** The first post must stand alone and make someone stop scrolling, in ≤280 characters. Lead with the result or a demo (a GIF/video of the thing working), not a link.

**Hard rules / mechanics** (2025–26 algorithm; see sources):
- **External links in the main post suppress reach** — the open-sourced ranking penalizes off-platform links by roughly 30–90%, and link-only posts from non-premium accounts can get near-zero reach.
- **Put the link in a reply.** Post the hook (and a thread, if you have more) natively; drop the link in the first reply where the penalty is far lighter.
- **A demo beats a description.** Native media (a short screen recording) earns the engagement the algorithm rewards.

**Format.** Post 1 = the hook + ideally a demo clip (≤280, no link). Posts 2+ = an optional thread (how it works, why you built it). Reply = the link.

**Timing.** Weekday mornings/lunch in your audience's timezone; engage with replies immediately.

**Do / don't.**
- Do: hook first, show a demo, link in the reply, reply to everyone.
- Don't: lead with a bare link, write a 280-char post that's all features, ask for retweets/likes.

Sources: [Do posts with links affect performance on X? (Buffer)](https://buffer.com/resources/links-on-x/), [How the X algorithm works 2026 (Sprout Social)](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/twitter-algorithm/).
